<p><p>Austempering is a heat treatment process used to improve the mechanical properties of steel, primarily through two market types: High-Temperature Austempering and Low-Temperature Austempering. High-Temperature Austempering typically involves heating steel to a temperature range that promotes austenite transformation, enhancing toughness and ductility. Conversely, Low-Temperature Austempering operates at lower temperatures, focusing on finer microstructures that increase wear resistance and hardness. Both methods cater to specific industrial applications, influencing the choice based on desired material characteristics and performance requirements.</p></p>
