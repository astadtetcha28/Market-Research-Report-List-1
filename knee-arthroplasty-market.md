<p><p>The knee arthroplasty market consists of three primary types: total knee arthroplasty, partial knee arthroplasty, and revision knee arthroplasty. Total knee arthroplasty involves replacing the entire knee joint, addressing severe arthritis or damage. Partial knee arthroplasty targets only the damaged compartment, offering a less invasive option for patients with localized issues. Revision knee arthroplasty is performed to repair or replace previously implanted knee prosthetics that have failed, ensuring improved function and pain relief for patients suffering from complications or wear.</p></p>
