<p><p>The nitrogen testing market comprises two primary types: instrument testing and chemical testing. Instrument testing utilizes sophisticated equipment, such as spectrometers and analyzers, to measure nitrogen levels accurately. This method is preferred for its precision and speed. In contrast, chemical testing involves traditional laboratory techniques, utilizing reagents to determine nitrogen content through chemical reactions. While chemical testing can be more cost-effective, it may lack the rapid results and accuracy of instrument testing, catering to different industry needs.</p></p>
