<p><p>Electricity transmission towers are categorized primarily into two market types: AC (Alternating Current) and DC (Direct Current). AC towers are used for transmitting electricity over shorter distances and are characterized by their ability to easily step voltage levels up or down using transformers. Conversely, DC towers are designed for long-distance transmission with lower energy loss and increased efficiency. The choice between AC and DC systems often depends on the distance, capacity, and specific requirements of the electrical grid infrastructure.</p></p>
