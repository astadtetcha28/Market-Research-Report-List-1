<p><p>The Cardiopulmonary Autotransfusion System (CATS) market is segmented into unwashed and washed ATS types. Unwashed ATS involves collecting blood during surgery and reinfusing it without any processing, retaining all cellular components. In contrast, washed ATS includes additional steps to eliminate contaminants and cellular debris, enhancing blood purity before reinfusion. While unwashed systems are simpler and more cost-effective, washed systems provide higher-quality reinfused blood, leading to better patient outcomes, making each type suitable for different clinical scenarios and preferences.</p></p>
